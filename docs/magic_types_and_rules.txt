Crúac
What it is: A bloody, witchcraft-like system of blood magic practiced primarily by the Circle of the Crone
. It harnesses the primal, consuming power of the vampire's Beast, forcing it to taint the world by invoking dark, ancient gods
.
How it works: It is a Ritual Discipline where spells are called "rites". Casting a rite costs one Vitae per dot of the rite
. The first point of Vitae is spent internally to fuel the Discipline, but any remaining Vitae must be physically spilled during the casting
. The caster makes an extended Manipulation + Occult + Crúac roll to cast the magic
.
How to acquire: A character must generally be an Acolyte in good standing, possessing at least one dot of Covenant Status (Circle of the Crone)
. It requires a teacher to learn, but does not require drinking the teacher's Vitae
. At character creation, if a player starts with Circle of the Crone Status, they may use their third Discipline dot to begin with Crúac
.
Drawbacks: Because it rouses the Beast, learning Crúac actively degrades a vampire's Humanity. Learning a dot of Crúac triggers a breaking point for vampires with Humanity 4 or higher
. Simply knowing the Discipline permanently caps the vampire's maximum Humanity at 10 minus their Crúac dots
. Additionally, vampire blood spilled during a rite becomes inert and unsuitable for feeding
.
Theban Sorcery
What it is: A system of dark miracles practiced by the Lancea et Sanctum, believed to be handed down from the angel Amoniel
. It is a stern, deliberate, and judgmental magic that transmits God's Curse to punish sinners and reveal truths
.
How it works: It is a Ritual Discipline where spells are called "miracles". Casting a miracle costs one Willpower point to mentally prepare, alongside a specific physical sacrament (a symbolic item) offered to saints or angelic forces
. Whether the ritual succeeds or fails, the physical sacrament crumbles to dust when the ritual reaches its crescendo
. The caster makes an extended Intelligence + Academics + Theban Sorcery roll
.
How to acquire: A character must be a priest with at least one dot of Covenant Status (Lancea et Sanctum) and swear oaths to never reveal the secrets to outsiders
. Like Crúac, it requires a teacher
, and starting characters with Status can use their third Discipline dot to learn it
.
Drawbacks: Theban Sorcery requires strict moral discipline. A Sanctified ritualist must possess a Humanity rating equal to or higher than the dot rating of the miracle they wish to cast
.
Kindred Necromancy
What it is: A highly focused, demoralizing death magic that relies on the lingering aura of death surrounding every vampire, rather than the power of the Blood or the Beast
.
How it works: It is a Ritual Discipline. Casting a necromantic ritual costs 1 Vitae plus a specific physical item
. This item serves either as a material focus (which the vampire keeps afterward) or a sacrifice (which is permanently lost)
. The caster makes a Resolve + Occult + Kindred Necromancy roll (though specific bloodlines might use alternative pools, like Presence + Persuasion or Composure + Occult)
.
How to acquire: Any vampire can learn it if they meet one of three conditions: 1) Finding a teacher, which limits their knowledge to their mentor's specific rituals; 2) Having a mortal cultural connection to death magic; or 3) Holding membership in a specific bloodline (like the Sangiovanni, Apollinaire, Burakumin, or Rexroth)
.
Drawbacks: Channeling this deathly energy draws the Kindred closer to death. Using a ritual triggers an automatic breaking point for any vampire with a Humanity of 7 or higher
. Furthermore, dots in Necromancy are added to the vampire's Blood Potency when calculating the duration they must spend in the dreaded sleep of torpor (up to a maximum effective Blood Potency of 10)
.
Mysteries of the Dragon (Coils and Scales)
What it is: Rather than traditional blood sorcery, the Ordo Dracul studies the Mysteries of the Dragon to transcend the vampiric condition, subverting the Curse through rigorous self-control, arcane chemistry, and clear-minded study
. The Mysteries are divided into two distinct mechanics: Coils and Scales
.
How Coils work: Coils are permanent, passive evolutionary alterations to the vampiric curse that manipulate the Beast and the Blood (e.g., conquering vulnerabilities to fire and sunlight, or mastering blood bonds)
. Unlike Disciplines or rituals, Coils do not drain a vampire's Vitae to use
.
How Scales work: Scales are active, ghastly procedures, experiments, and surgeries used to share or weaponize the effects of Coils on others
. Through Scales, a Dragon can force others to experience their enlightened state—such as making vampires sleep at night, giving mortals a taste for blood, or permanently altering ghouls
. Every Scale resonates with a specific tier of a specific Coil
.
How to acquire: Upon joining the Ordo Dracul, a vampire must commit to a single primary Mystery
. They can learn Coils outside their chosen Mystery, but they can have no more dots in a non-Mystery Coil than they possess dots in the Ordo Dracul Status Merit
. Like other covenant arts, characters starting with Status can use their third Discipline dot on Coils
. Learning a Scale costs 1 Experience if you possess the prerequisite Coil, and 2 Experiences if you do not
.
Drawbacks: Developing new Scales is incredibly difficult, taking months of extended research rolls and requiring test subjects, occult texts, and a safe laboratory
. Because these procedures are brutal and tantamount to torture, developing a new Scale (whether it succeeds or fails) inflicts a breaking point at Humanity 5 minus the dot rating of the prerequisite Coil
