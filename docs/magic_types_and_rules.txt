Rituals are the overarching mechanical and narrative actions used to harness any of the Ritual Disciplines, such as Crúac, Theban Sorcery, Kindred Necromancy, or Triadic Evolution
. A ritual is the actual step-by-step process of casting the magic: it requires the vampire to physically prepare a sacrifice, coax forth the power of the Beast or the divine, and systematically shape it over an extended period of time to achieve a specific magical effect
.
Rites are the specific, in-world terms for the individual powers or spells cast by certain pagan or feral covenants, most notably the Circle of the Crone
. The term "rite" is also adopted by bloodlines that practice similar terrestrial or pagan blood sorceries, such as the Lithopedia rites used by the Család bloodline
. Essentially, all rites are rituals, but not all rituals are rites.
Crúac
Overview: A bloody, witchcraft-like system of blood magic practiced primarily by the Circle of the Crone
. It harnesses the primal, consuming power of the vampire's Beast, forcing it to taint the world by invoking the dark gods of the Crone
.
Cost: Casting a Crúac rite costs one Vitae per dot of the rite
. The first point of Vitae is spent internally to fuel the Discipline, but any remaining Vitae must be physically spilled during the casting
.
Drawbacks: Because it rouses the Beast, learning Crúac actively degrades a vampire's Humanity. Learning a dot of Crúac is a breaking point for vampires with Humanity 4 or higher, and simply knowing the Discipline permanently caps the vampire's Humanity at 10 minus their Crúac dots
.
Theban Sorcery
Overview: A system of dark miracles practiced by the Lancea et Sanctum
. Believed to have been passed down from an angel named Amoniel, it is a stern, deliberate, and judgmental magic that transmits the touch of God's Curse to punish sinners and reveal truths
. Its spells are called "miracles"
.
Cost: Casting a Theban miracle requires the caster to spend one Willpower point to mentally prepare, alongside a specific physical sacrament (a symbolic item) offered to saints or angelic forces
. Regardless of whether the ritual succeeds or fails, the physical sacrament crumbles to dust at the end of the attempt
.
Drawbacks: Theban Sorcery requires strict moral discipline; a Sanctified ritualist must possess a Humanity rating equal to or higher than the dot rating of the miracle they wish to perform
.
Kindred Necromancy
Overview: A highly focused and demoralizing death magic that relies not on the power of the Blood or the Beast, but on the lingering aura of death surrounding every vampire
. It is inherently practiced by specific bloodlines (such as the Sangiovanni, Apollinaire, Burakumin, and Rexroth)
, though any vampire can learn it if they find a teacher or possess a mortal cultural connection to death magic
.
Cost: Casting a necromantic ritual costs 1 Vitae plus a specific required physical item
. This item serves either as a material focus (which the vampire keeps afterward) or a sacrifice (which is permanently lost)
.
Drawbacks: Channeling this deathly energy draws the Kindred closer to death. Using a ritual triggers an automatic breaking point for any vampire with a Humanity of 7 or higher
. Additionally, dots in Necromancy are added to the vampire's Blood Potency when calculating the duration they must spend in torpor, up to a maximum effective Blood Potency of 10
.
Mysteries of the Dragon Instead of traditional blood sorcery, the Ordo Dracul studies the Mysteries of the Dragon
. Their goal is to completely transcend the vampiric condition, subverting the Curse through rigorous self-control, arcane chemistry, and clear-minded study
. Upon joining the covenant, a vampire commits to a primary Mystery (such as the Ascendant, the Wyrm, or the Voivode)
. The Mysteries are divided into two distinct mechanics:
Coils: Coils are permanent, passive evolutionary alterations to the vampiric curse that manipulate the Beast and the Blood to the vampire's benefit
. Through Coils, a Dragon can conquer vulnerabilities to fire and sunlight, tame their frenzies, or master blood bonds
. Unlike Disciplines or rituals, Coils do not drain a vampire's Vitae
.
Scales: Scales are active, ghastly procedures, experiments, and surgeries used to share or weaponize the effects of Coils
. Through Scales, a Dragon can force others to experience their enlightened state—such as making vampires sleep at night and wake during the day, giving mortals a taste for blood, or permanently altering the bodies of ghouls
. Every Scale resonates with a specific tier of a specific Coil
. If the vampire possesses the prerequisite Coil, the Scale costs only 1 Experience to learn; if they do not, it costs 2 Experiences
