Disciplines at Character Creation When creating a new character, you receive three dots to allocate to Disciplines

At least two dots must be spent on the vampire's in-clan Disciplines

The third dot may be spent on any Discipline
 If you choose a non-clan Discipline, you must justify how your character learned it, such as having a mentor or stealing blood from another clan

If your character begins play with the Covenant Status Merit in the Circle of the Crone, Lancea et Sanctum, or Ordo Dracul, you may use this third dot to begin with Crúac, Theban Sorcery, or the Coils of the Dragon, respectively
 With Storyteller permission, you can also use this third dot to start with these covenant powers without having Status, though this implies you stole the secrets and may be hunted for it

Disciplines with Prerequisites Many Disciplines carry strict prerequisites for learning and advancing:
Out-of-Clan Disciplines: While any Kindred can independently develop Animalism, Celerity, Obfuscate, Resilience, and Vigor, learning Auspex, Dominate, Majesty, Nightmare, or Protean outside of your clan requires a teacher
 You must drink at least one point of Vitae from a vampire who possesses the Discipline at a higher level than you are trying to learn

Covenant Disciplines: Crúac, Theban Sorcery, and the Coils of the Dragon require a teacher to learn, but do not require the student to drink their Vitae
 Generally, you must possess at least one dot of Covenant Status in the respective covenant to learn them

Theban Sorcery: Because miracles require deliberate exercise of intellect and faith, a Sanctified ritualist must possess a Humanity rating equal to or higher than the dot rating of the miracle they wish to perform

Crúac: Learning a dot of Crúac is a breaking point for vampires with Humanity 4 or higher. Additionally, simply knowing the Discipline permanently caps the vampire's Humanity at 10 minus their Crúac dots

Bloodline Disciplines: Bloodline Disciplines are fiercely restricted. Nobody outside the bloodline can learn their unique gift, not even through diablerie
 To learn them, a vampire must formally join the bloodline, which typically requires reaching Blood Potency 2 and feeding from an Avus (a mentor from the bloodline with whom they share a blood tie)

Kindred Necromancy: Any vampire can learn this Discipline, but they must meet one of three specific conditions: finding a teacher (which limits their knowledge to their mentor's rituals), having a cultural connection to death magic from their mortal life, or being a member of a specific bloodline (such as the Sangiovanni or Burakumin)
